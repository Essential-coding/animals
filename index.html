    Different animals

    Camel

    A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back. Camels have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food and textiles.

    Snow Leopard

    The Snow Leopards powerful build allows it to travel quickly. Its tail provide great balance and agility and also protects it from the cold. The Snow Leopard is found in twelve countries, stretching from central asia to south asia.

    Lion

    Lions live in a variety of habitats but prefer grassland, savanna, dense scrub, and open woodland. The African lions are the most social cats of all the cats around the globe.

    Golden Eagle

    Golden Eagles are considered one of the best fliers amongst all birds. It can travel with a speed of 320km/h and they use their sharp talons to hunt for rabbits and other animals like fish.

    Why animals matter

    Animals are important for our environment. We are dependent of the animals and their materials. We use them for food and for traveling as well as for fur and other material things. We even use some of them for hunting food and for protection. They are also a part of the food chain which is crucial for the biological world that we live in.
